Pumpkin Sausage Soup

Creamy pumpkin sausage soup with mushrooms, pepperjack, and cheddar cheese

When it comes to fall I am ALL ABOUT everything pumpkin. My wife gets tired of it much quicker than me, but this soup is a game changer. The pumpkin is much more subtle than other pumpkin-based recipes. The combo of pumpkin, italian sausage, and cheddar/pepperjack makes this recipe so rich and savory. It's a hit with pumpkin lovers and haters alike.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of Italian sausage
  • 2 cups of sliced mushrooms
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 32oz box of chicken stock
  • 1 15oz can of pumpkin
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tsp Italian seasoning, or try this Homemade Italian Seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1/3 cup cold water
  • 1/3 cup cornstarch
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded pepperjack cheese

Steps

  1. In a dutch oven or cast iron combo cooker cook the sausage, mushrooms and onion over medium high heat until sausage is no longer pink and vegetables are tender, about 8-10 minutes, breaking up the sausage into crumbles; drain. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add chicken stock, pumpkin, sugar, and seasonings. Bring to a boil then reduce heat. Cover and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Stir in cream. In a small bowl mix the cold water and cornstarch until smooth. Quickly stir into pan before they separate. Bring to a boil; cook and stir until thickened, about 1-2 minutes. Add the cheese and cook/stir until the cheese is melted.
